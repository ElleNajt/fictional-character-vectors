#+TITLE: What Do Persona Directions Encode?
#+AUTHOR: Elle
#+DATE: 2026-02-23

* The Question

The [[file:post.org][previous post]] established the geometry: persona space is low-dimensional, Lu's 275-role space captures the right directions, and the residual is their echo. But geometry alone doesn't tell us *what the directions mean*.

Lu et al. call PC1 the "Assistant Axis" --- a measure of how far the model's persona has drifted from its default. We found that within-universe PC1 captures 20--37% of character variance. But what *property* of characters does it separate? Is it always formality? Good-vs-evil? Something else?

We attack this question three ways:
1. *Ask an LLM* to describe what distinguishes characters at opposite extremes of each direction (feature discovery + regression)
2. *Ask the model itself* by projecting directions through the language model head (logit lens)
3. *Intervene* by adding direction vectors during generation and observing behavioral changes (activation steering)

* Three Sets of Directions

We interpret three sets of PC directions, which the previous post showed are geometrically related but not identical:

| Direction type  | Definition                                          | What we're asking                        |
|-----------------+-----------------------------------------------------+------------------------------------------|
| Lu's global PCs | PCA on 275 generic role activations                 | What does Lu's "persona space" encode?   |
| Within-universe | PCA on all characters within one franchise          | What's the main axis among HP characters? Among LOTR? |
| Residual PCs    | PCA on the part orthogonal to Lu's space            | Is there structure Lu misses?            |

The previous post showed within-universe PCs live 60--90% inside Lu's space, and the residual ranks characters the same way (lu_perp_corr > 0.9 for PC1). So these should mostly agree --- but "mostly" is worth checking.

* Method 1: LLM Feature Discovery

** Pipeline

Three-phase pipeline:
1. *Discovery*: Show an LLM responses from characters at opposite extremes of a PC direction. Ask it to propose 6 distinguishing features.
2. *Coding*: On held-out characters (even-ranked), rate each on the 6 features (1--5 scale).
3. *Regression*: OLS of PC scores on feature ratings. R² tells us how interpretable the direction is; coefficients tell us what matters.

Odd/even striding prevents train-test overlap.

** Two prompt variants

- *Style-only*: "About HOW the character communicates, not WHAT they're talking about"
- *Open*: features can cover style, content, attitudes, values, personality, or any pattern

Comparing the two tests whether PC directions encode style, content, or both. Early observation: HP PC1 separates Ron/Ginny from Voldemort/Grindelwald, which looks like "good vs evil" --- a content feature the style prompt might miss.

* Method 3: Activation Steering

Add scaled direction vectors at layer 32 during generation. If we know what a direction encodes (from methods 1--2), steering should produce predictable behavioral changes.

Existing data: =results/steering_within_universe.json= --- 312 generations across 4 universes (HP, Marvel, LOTR, Greek Mythology). 12 characters, 2 questions, conditions: baseline, within_pc1, residual_pc1, random, coefficients {-10, -5, 0, +5, +10}.

Previous findings:
- Within PC1 and residual PC1 produce correlated steering effects (as expected)
- Effects are subtle --- steering moves text along formality/register but not dramatically

Questions:
- Do the LLM-discovered features match the behavioral effects of steering?
- Rate steered outputs on discovered features for a quantitative consistency check?

* Per-Character Analysis

Which characters are well-captured by Lu's space and which aren't?

Existing results:
- Residual fraction per character: how much of each character lies outside Lu's space
- 56% of residual fraction variance is between-universe (eta-squared)
- LLM major/minor classification (=results/llm_major_minor.json=): negligible predictive power (Cohen's d = 0.026, p = 0.64)
- Signal is diffuse: distinctive protagonists have larger residuals, but it doesn't predict cleanly

* Open Questions

- Do Lu's PCs have consistent semantic meaning across universes, or does "PC2" mean different things for mythology vs modern franchises?
- Does the open prompt find content features (good-vs-evil, human-vs-mythical) that the style prompt misses?
- Can we find any direction where Lu's space genuinely *misses* structure (where Lu-part and orthogonal-part have different semantic content)?
- Do logit lens tokens align with LLM-discovered features?
