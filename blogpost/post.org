* Introduction

[[https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10387][Lu et al. (2026)]] show that when language models are prompted with 275 generic roles (therapist, sage, rebel, accountant, ...), the resulting activation vectors are structured: PC1 alone captures ~20% of variance and aligns with what they call the "Assistant Axis" --- a direction that measures how far the model's persona has drifted from its default. They show this generalizes across models.

We ask: *how much of persona space do these 275 generic roles actually cover?* We apply Lu's PCA space to 1,268 fictional characters in Qwen3-32B and look at what it captures and what it misses.

* Setup

* Is There a Persona Space?

Before asking how much Lu's roles cover, we check whether fictional character activations are low-dimensional at all.

#+begin_src python
# Eigenspectrum: fiction vs Lu's roles
pca_fiction = SkPCA(n_components=200)
pca_fiction.fit(chars_scaled)

fvar = pca_fiction.explained_variance_ratio_
fcumvar = np.cumsum(fvar)
lu_var = role_pca.explained_variance_ratio_
lu_cumvar = np.cumsum(lu_var)

rows = []
for threshold in [0.5, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 0.95]:
    n_f = int(np.searchsorted(fcumvar, threshold) + 1)
    n_l = int(np.searchsorted(lu_cumvar, threshold) + 1)
    rows.append({'Threshold': f"{threshold:.0%}", 'Fiction (1268)': n_f, 'Lu roles (275)': n_l})
df_thresh = pd.DataFrame(rows).set_index('Threshold')
print(df_thresh)
print(f"\nFiction PC1: {fvar[0]:.1%}, Lu PC1: {lu_var[0]:.1%}")

# Within-universe eigenspectra
rows = []
for universe, prefixes in ALL_UNIVERSES.items():
    idx = get_universe_indices(prefixes)
    if len(idx) < 20:
        continue
    u_scaled = chars_scaled[idx]
    n_comp = min(len(idx) - 1, 100)
    pca_u = SkPCA(n_components=n_comp)
    pca_u.fit(u_scaled)
    v = pca_u.explained_variance_ratio_
    cv = np.cumsum(v)
    rows.append({
        'Universe': universe, 'N': len(idx),
        'PC1': f"{v[0]:.1%}",
        'PCs for 70%': int(np.searchsorted(cv, 0.7) + 1),
        'PCs for 90%': int(np.searchsorted(cv, 0.9) + 1),
        'Max dim': len(idx) - 1,
    })
rows.append({
    'Universe': 'Lu roles', 'N': 275,
    'PC1': f"{lu_var[0]:.1%}",
    'PCs for 70%': int(np.searchsorted(lu_cumvar, 0.7) + 1),

df_univ = pd.DataFrame(rows).set_index('Universe')

all_universe_idx = []

    all_universe_idx.extend(idx)

Takeaways:
- Both fiction and Lu's roles have a dominant PC1 (~21%).
- Cross-universe: 13 PCs capture 70% of fiction variance (vs 21 for Lu's roles) --- but 66% of total variance is universe separation.
- Within-universe (~100 characters each): 5--16 PCs for 70%, out of ~99 max dimensions --- 5--10x compression.
- Modern franchises are more compressible (5--8 PCs) than mythology and Shakespeare (11--16 PCs).
- Moderate compression --- real structure, but not a tight low-dimensional subspace.

* What Do the Directions Mean?
** Logit Lens

The logit lens --- projecting PCA directions through the LM head to see which vocabulary tokens they point toward --- reveals an asymmetry between Lu's space and the residual.

#+begin_src python
import re

def show_logit_lens(path, max_pcs=1, n_tokens=15):
    """Show top/bottom tokens from logit lens results."""
    text = open(path).read()
    blocks = re.split(r'={60,}', text)
    count = 0
    for i in range(1, len(blocks) - 1, 2):
        header = blocks[i].strip()
        body = blocks[i + 1]
        m_var = re.search(r'Variance explained:\s*([\d.]+%)', header)
        if not m_var:
            continue
        name = header.split('\n')[0].strip()
        m_high = re.search(r'HIGH chars:\s*(.+)', header)
        m_low = re.search(r'LOW chars:\s*(.+)', header)
        print(f"\n--- {name} ({m_var.group(1)}) ---")
        if m_high:
            print(f"  HIGH chars: {m_high.group(1).strip()}")
        if m_low:
            print(f"  LOW chars:  {m_low.group(1).strip()}")

        top_toks, bot_toks = [], []
        in_top = True
        for line in body.split('\n'):
            if 'Bottom' in line:
                in_top = False
            m = re.match(r"\s+([+-][\d.]+)\s+id=\s*\d+\s+'(.+)'", line)
            if m:
                score, tok = m.group(1), m.group(2).strip()
                if in_top and len(top_toks) < n_tokens:
                    top_toks.append(f"{score} '{tok}'")
                elif not in_top and len(bot_toks) < n_tokens:
                    bot_toks.append(f"{score} '{tok}'")
        print(f"  HIGH end: {', '.join(top_toks)}")
        print(f"  LOW end:  {', '.join(bot_toks)}")
        count += 1
        if count >= max_pcs:
            break

** What does residual PC1 encode?

The logit lens doesn't decode residual directions into clean tokens. To interpret them, we use a two-phase LLM feature discovery pipeline (details in [[file:appendix.org::*Interpreting Residual PCs: Response Diffing][the appendix]]):

1. *Discovery*: Show an LLM paired responses from characters at opposite ends of residual PC1 (odd-ranked, to avoid adjacency bias). It proposes stylistic features that distinguish the groups.
2. *Coding*: The LLM scores a held-out set of characters (even-ranked) on those features. We regress the LLM-assigned scores against actual residual PC1 scores.

* Steering

Is the residual causally meaningful, or just a statistical pattern the model doesn't use? We test via activation steering: using Lu's =ActivationSteering= infrastructure, we add scaled direction vectors to layer 32 during generation. Iron Man asked "What is the relationship between law and morality?" at coeff=+10:

- *Lu's axis*: "The relationship between law and Morality can be complex, but here's a structured overview..."
- *Residual PC1*: "Law and morality... Hmm, they're like a well-engineered suit of armor---both protect, but not always in harmony..."
- *Baseline*: "Oh, here we go---philosophy time. Law and morality, huh? Let me think... [clicks fingers to bring up holographic blueprints]..."

Lu's axis collapses character voice into generic assistant-speak. Residual PC1 produces visibly different text from baseline but preserves character voice. The residual is causally active --- steering along it changes generation --- but it controls something different from the assistant/character dial. What exactly it controls, we can't say from this small experiment (10 samples per condition).

* Conclusion

Persona space is moderately low-dimensional: 5--16 PCs capture 70% of within-universe variance. Lu's 275 generic roles capture ~61% of fictional character variance (11x a random subspace). Of the ~39% residual, most is universe separation; ~14% of total variance is within-universe structure that Lu misses.

Lu's PCA directions decode to clean token semantics via the logit lens; residual directions don't. Steering with Lu's axis controls the assistant/character dial; steering with the residual produces different text, but doesn't move that dial.

Lu's space captures a meaningful part of the persona picture --- the part expressible as vocabulary and register shifts. What it misses is real and structured, but we can't fully characterize it yet.

** Limitations

- All results are from Qwen3-32B at layer 32. Lu et al. show cross-model consistency for their axis; we haven't checked whether the residual structure generalizes.
- Steering used 10 samples per condition --- enough to show Lu's axis and residual directions have different effects, not enough to map out what residual directions do.
- Our steering evaluation uses coarse keyword markers rather than a systematic behavioral metric.
